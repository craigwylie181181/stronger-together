Sustainability

We’re not going to call ourselves a sustainable brand — the fashion industry has a complicated relationship with that word, and we’d rather be honest about what we’re doing and where we’re falling short.

What We’re Doing Right

  • Recycled fibres: Our Friday and Foundation lines use 72–78% recycled polyester and nylon, sourced from post-consumer plastic waste
  • European manufacturing: All production happens in Porto, Portugal — short supply chains, EU labour standards, lower shipping emissions
  • Plastic-free packaging: Every order ships in a compostable mailer with recycled tissue paper. No polybags, no plastic tape
  • Small batches: We produce in small runs to avoid overstock. If something sells out, we recut — we’d rather you wait a week than send unsold inventory to landfill
  • Durability as sustainability: Our fabrics are tested to 100+ washes. The most sustainable garment is the one you don’t have to replace

Where We’re Not There Yet

  • Elastane: There’s currently no commercially viable recycled elastane at the quality we need for compression activewear. We use virgin elastane (22–28% of each garment) and are watching the material science closely
  • Shipping emissions: We don’t yet offset shipping carbon. We’re evaluating partners but haven’t found one we trust to deliver real offsets
  • Take-back programme: We want to build a garment return and recycling programme but don’t have the infrastructure yet. It’s on our 2027 roadmap

Our Promise

We’ll keep improving, and we’ll keep being honest about what we haven’t figured out. If you have ideas or questions, reach out at hello@strongertogether.ae.
